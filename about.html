About Maegan Jong

Maegan is currently a first-year student at Harvard University. She is interested in leveraging technology and data to create lasting change. Her love for service developed throughout her time in high school.

In 2016, Maegan founded the mental health nonprofit Nomi Bobi and began her mental health advocacy efforts. Through sales of handmade Nomi Owl decorations, she raised funds to support both mental health organizations in the US and the artists in Taiwan who made them. Maegan soon grew her impact and brought mental health awareness to her high school. Collaborating with administrators, she initiated and organized a schoolwide Mental Health Presentation Day event for over 2,700 high school students. She further contributed to the cause by conducting research. During her time at Texas Tech University as a Clark Scholar, Maegan determined the effects of social context on the behavior of individuals with anxiety.

Committed to spreading knowledge for the benefit of her community, Maegan designed and taught a Chinese language and culture curriculum for seniors at a local retirement center. Featured in Daily Herald and Patch, Maegan grew closer to not only her students but also her heritage.

In her free time, Maegan can be found coding recreationally, testing recipes, and getting lost in nature.
